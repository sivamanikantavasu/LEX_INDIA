set 8:
1. The Preamble of the Indian Constitution was amended by which Constitutional Amendment?

A) 24th Amendment
B) 42nd Amendment
C) 44th Amendment
D) 52nd Amendment

Correct Answer: B) 42nd Amendment

Explanation:
The 42nd Constitutional Amendment Act (1976) amended the Preamble by adding the words “Socialist,” “Secular,” and “Integrity.”

2. Which Article provides for the establishment of the Union Public Service Commission (UPSC)?

A) Article 315
B) Article 320
C) Article 324
D) Article 338

Correct Answer: A) Article 315

Explanation:
Article 315 provides for the establishment of Public Service Commissions for the Union and States.

3. Which writ is issued to a public authority to perform a public duty?

A) Certiorari
B) Prohibition
C) Mandamus
D) Quo Warranto

Correct Answer: C) Mandamus

Explanation:
Mandamus means “we command.” It directs a public authority to perform its legal duty.

4. The Constitution of India describes India as which type of democracy?

A) Direct Democracy
B) Presidential Democracy
C) Parliamentary Democracy
D) Monarchical Democracy

Correct Answer: C) Parliamentary Democracy

Explanation:
India follows the Parliamentary system of democracy similar to the United Kingdom.

5. The resignation of the Vice-President is addressed to:

A) President
B) Prime Minister
C) Speaker of Lok Sabha
D) Chief Justice

Correct Answer: A) President

Explanation:
The Vice-President resigns by writing to the President.

6. Which Article deals with the President’s power to issue Ordinances?

A) Article 121
B) Article 123
C) Article 124
D) Article 125

Correct Answer: B) Article 123

Explanation:
Article 123 empowers the President to promulgate Ordinances when Parliament is not in session.

7. The power to levy taxes is derived from:

A) Article 256
B) Article 265
C) Article 300
D) Article 360

Correct Answer: B) Article 265

Explanation:
Article 265 states that no tax shall be levied or collected except by authority of law.

8. The Constitution provides for how many types of emergencies?

A) One
B) Two
C) Three
D) Four

Correct Answer: C) Three

Explanation:
The Constitution provides for National Emergency (Article 352), State Emergency (Article 356), and Financial Emergency (Article 360).

9. Which Article deals with Cultural and Educational Rights of minorities?

A) Article 27
B) Article 29
C) Article 30
D) Both B and C

Correct Answer: D) Both B and C

Explanation:
Articles 29 and 30 protect the cultural and educational rights of minorities.

10. The concept of a Welfare State is reflected in:

A) Fundamental Rights
B) Directive Principles of State Policy
C) Fundamental Duties
D) Preamble only

Correct Answer: B) Directive Principles of State Policy

Explanation:
DPSPs guide the state to promote social and economic welfare.

11. The Chief Justice of a High Court retires at the age of:

A) 60
B) 62
C) 65
D) 67

Correct Answer: B) 62

Explanation:
High Court judges retire at 62 years of age.

12. The concept of Suspension of Fundamental Rights during Emergency is under:

A) Article 358
B) Article 360
C) Article 300
D) Article 280

Correct Answer: A) Article 358

Explanation:
Article 358 deals with suspension of Article 19 rights during National Emergency.

13. The Indian Constitution is regarded as:

A) Rigid
B) Flexible
C) Both Rigid and Flexible
D) Unwritten

Correct Answer: C) Both Rigid and Flexible

Explanation:
The Constitution contains both rigid and flexible amendment procedures.

14. The Governor’s Ordinance-making power is under:

A) Article 200
B) Article 213
C) Article 220
D) Article 240

Correct Answer: B) Article 213

Explanation:
Article 213 empowers the Governor to issue Ordinances when the State Legislature is not in session.

15. Which Article deals with the Election of the President?

A) Article 52
B) Article 54
C) Article 58
D) Article 61

Correct Answer: B) Article 54

Explanation:
Article 54 provides for the election of the President by an electoral college.
